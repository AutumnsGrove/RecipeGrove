# Tonkotsu Ramen ![üçú+‚≠ê](emojikitchen/1f35c_2b50.png)

Rich Japanese pork bone broth with tender noodles and toppings.

## Ingredients

### For the Broth
- 2 lbs pork bones (neck bones or trotters)
- 1 lb pork belly
- 6 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 onion, quartered
- 2-inch piece ginger, sliced
- 2 green onions

### For the Toppings
- 4 soft-boiled eggs (marinated in soy sauce overnight)
- 1 lb fresh ramen noodles
- 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
- 1 cup bamboo shoots
- 4 green onions, sliced
- Sesame seeds
- Chili oil

### For the Tare (Seasoning)
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 2 tbsp mirin
- 1 tbsp sake

## Instructions

1. Blanch pork bones in boiling water for 5 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.
2. Place bones in a large pot with 12 cups water, bring to a rolling boil.
3. Boil vigorously for 4-6 hours, adding water as needed to keep bones covered. ![‚ô®Ô∏è+üî•](emojikitchen/2668_1f525.png)
4. Add pork belly in the last hour of cooking.
5. Strain broth, pressing on solids. The broth should be milky white.
6. Slice cooked pork belly thinly and set aside.
7. Mix tare ingredients in a small bowl.
8. Cook ramen noodles according to package directions. ![üçú+üòä](emojikitchen/1f35c_1f60a.png)
9. To serve: Add 2 tbsp tare to each bowl, ladle in hot broth.
10. Add noodles, top with sliced pork belly, soft-boiled egg, bamboo shoots, nori, and green onions. ![ü•¢+‚≠ê](emojikitchen/1f962_2b50.png)
11. Drizzle with chili oil and sprinkle sesame seeds.

## Notes ![üçñ+‚ù§Ô∏è](emojikitchen/1f356_1f60a.png)

Traditional tonkotsu takes 12+ hours of simmering. This version is shortened but still delivers rich, creamy broth. The key is maintaining a vigorous boil to emulsify the fats.
