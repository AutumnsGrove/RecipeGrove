## Ingredients

### For the Marinade
- 3 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
- 2 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
- 1/4 cup white vinegar
- 1/4 cup pineapple juice
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- Salt and pepper

### For the Tacos
- 2 lbs pork shoulder, thinly sliced
- 1 fresh pineapple, sliced
- 1 white onion, diced
- Fresh cilantro, chopped
- Corn tortillas
- Lime wedges

## Instructions

1. Toast the dried chiles in a dry skillet for 2-3 minutes until fragrant. ![ğŸŒ¶ï¸+ğŸ”¥](emojikitchen/1f336_1f525.png)
2. Soak chiles in hot water for 15 minutes, then drain.
3. Blend soaked chiles with vinegar, pineapple juice, garlic, and spices until smooth.
4. Marinate pork slices in the chile mixture for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
5. Grill pineapple slices until charred, then dice.
6. Cook marinated pork on high heat until edges are crispy and caramelized. ![ğŸ–+ğŸ”¥](emojikitchen/1f356_1f525.png)
7. Warm tortillas on the grill.
8. Assemble tacos with pork, grilled pineapple, onion, and cilantro. ![ğŸŒ®+â­](emojikitchen/1f32e_2b50.png)
9. Serve with lime wedges.

## Notes ![ğŸŒ®+ğŸ˜Š](emojikitchen/1f32e_1f60a.png)

Traditional al pastor is cooked on a vertical spit (trompo), but this version adapts well for home cooking. The key is getting good caramelization on the pork.
