# Pho Bo (Vietnamese Beef Noodle Soup)

Aromatic Vietnamese beef broth with rice noodles and fresh herbs.

## Ingredients

### For the Broth
- 3 lbs beef bones (knuckle and marrow bones)
- 2 lbs beef brisket or chuck
- 1 large onion, halved
- 4-inch piece ginger, halved lengthwise
- 4 star anise
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 3 cloves
- 1 tbsp coriander seeds
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp sugar
- Salt to taste

### For Serving
- 1 lb dried rice noodles (banh pho)
- 1/2 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced (for raw topping)
- Bean sprouts
- Fresh basil
- Fresh cilantro
- Fresh mint
- Lime wedges

## Instructions

1. Char onion and ginger halves under broiler or over open flame until blackened.
2. Blanch beef bones in boiling water for 5 minutes, then rinse.
3. Place bones and brisket in a large pot with 16 cups water.
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
5. Toast star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and coriander seeds in a dry pan.
6. Add charred onion, ginger, and toasted spices to broth.
7. Simmer for 3-4 hours, skimming foam regularly.
8. Remove brisket after 2 hours, let cool, then slice thinly.
9. Strain broth, season with fish sauce, sugar, and salt.
10. Soak rice noodles in hot water until soft, then drain.
11. To serve: Place noodles in bowls, top with sliced cooked brisket and raw beef slices.
12. Ladle boiling hot broth over (this will cook the raw beef).
13. Serve with herb plate, lime wedges, and condiments.

## Serving

Each person customizes their bowl with herbs, bean sprouts, lime juice, and chili sauce. The broth should be clear and aromatic with a complex spice profile.

## Tips

The key to great pho is a clear, flavorful broth. Don't let it boil too hard, and skim regularly. The raw beef should be sliced paper-thin against the grain.
