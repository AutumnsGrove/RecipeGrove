# Falafel ![ğŸ§†+â­](emojikitchen/1f9c6_2b50.png)

Crispy Middle Eastern chickpea fritters.

## Ingredients

- 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight, not canned)
- 1 small onion, roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 cup fresh parsley
- 1 cup fresh cilantro
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp coriander
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 2 tbsp flour
- Salt and pepper
- Vegetable oil for frying

### For Serving
- Pita bread
- Tahini sauce
- Chopped tomatoes
- Cucumber
- Pickled turnips
- Lettuce

## Instructions

1. Drain soaked chickpeas thoroughly and pat dry.
2. Add chickpeas, onion, garlic, herbs, and spices to food processor.
3. Pulse until mixture is finely ground but not a paste. It should hold together when squeezed.
4. Transfer to a bowl, stir in baking powder and flour.
5. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or up to overnight).
6. Heat 3 inches of oil in a deep pot to 350Â°F. ![ğŸ«•+ğŸ”¥](emojikitchen/1fad5_1f525.png)
7. Form mixture into small balls or patties (about 2 tablespoons each).
8. Fry in batches for 3-4 minutes until deep golden brown.
9. Drain on paper towels.
10. Serve hot in pita bread with tahini sauce and fresh vegetables.

## Tips ![ğŸ’¡+â­](emojikitchen/1f4a1_2b50.png)

**Critical:** Use dried chickpeas that have been soaked, NOT canned. Canned chickpeas have too much moisture and will make soggy falafel. The mixture should hold together when squeezed but still be slightly crumbly.

## Tahini Sauce

Mix 1/2 cup tahini, 1/4 cup lemon juice, 2 cloves minced garlic, and water to thin. Season with salt.
