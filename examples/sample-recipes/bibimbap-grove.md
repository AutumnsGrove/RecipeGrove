Korean mixed rice bowl with vegetables, meat, and gochujang.

## Ingredients

### For the Bowl
- 4 cups cooked short-grain white rice
- 8 oz beef (ribeye or sirloin), thinly sliced
- 2 cups spinach
- 1 cup bean sprouts
- 1 cup julienned carrots
- 1 cup julienned zucchini
- 8 oz shiitake mushrooms, sliced
- 4 eggs
- Sesame oil
- Sesame seeds
- Nori strips (optional)

### For the Beef Marinade
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp grated ginger

### For the Gochujang Sauce
- 3 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tsp minced garlic

## Instructions

1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, garlic, and ginger for 20 minutes.
2. Prepare vegetables separately:
   - Blanch spinach, squeeze dry, season with sesame oil and salt
   - Blanch bean sprouts, drain well
   - Saut√© carrots with a pinch of salt until tender
   - Saut√© zucchini with a pinch of salt
   - Saut√© mushrooms until golden
3. Cook marinated beef in a hot pan until browned.
4. Fry eggs sunny-side up with slightly runny yolks.
5. Mix gochujang sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
6. To assemble: Place rice in bowls, arrange vegetables and beef in sections on top.
7. Top each bowl with a fried egg.
8. Drizzle with gochujang sauce, sprinkle with sesame seeds.
9. Mix everything together before eating.

## Notes ![üçö+üî•](emojikitchen/1f35a_1f525.png)

"Bibimbap" means "mixed rice." The key is having everything at the right temperature - warm rice, hot vegetables and meat, and a runny egg that coats everything when mixed. Traditionally served in a hot stone bowl (dolsot) that creates a crispy rice crust.
